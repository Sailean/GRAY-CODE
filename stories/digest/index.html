Nonlinear Emotion Curves

I. June 13, 2010

When he woke up, Emiko was still sleeping beside him.

—Ⅰ came back.

Various sounds filtered through the window.

A parked bus opening its doors.

The hiss of compressed air releasing from the engine's air cylinders, followed by the momentary vacuum—that sound and its lingering resonance.

Akira had always loved this sound—how the swollen envelope of noise instantly decayed toward silence.

A child's voice calling "Mom" overlapped with the high-pitched engine of a bus departing from the stop.

Carried by June's humid air, the sound of pedestrian signals arrived from afar, intermittently interrupted by the flow of cars and waves of people, then resurfacing again. And... the low, quiet vibration of the air conditioner against the walls of the room.

Akira propped himself up on his left elbow, supporting his head, and adjusted the blanket over Emiko's bare shoulder.

—And today... I'll be going back. To that dry city.

Akira let out a small sigh and gently brushed the hair away from his lover's cheeks and forehead.

His gaze moved across her face, searching for characteristic points, analyzing her expression.

—The angle of her eyebrows and eye corners... the change in her mouth corners... Action Units.
